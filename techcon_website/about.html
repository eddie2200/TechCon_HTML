History

TechCon was founded in 2012 as a grassroots gathering of software developers, tech entrepreneurs, and digital creatives in Nairobi, Kenya. What began as a one-day local meetup has since grown into one of the most influential technology conferences in East Africa. Over the past decade, TechCon has evolved into a dynamic platform that showcases emerging technologies, fosters innovation, and connects diverse voices across the tech ecosystem—from students and startups to global industry leaders.

Mission

The mission of TechCon is to empower the next generation of African technologists by creating inclusive, forward-thinking spaces for collaboration, learning, and innovation. We believe technology is a powerful tool for solving real-world problems, and our goal is to spotlight homegrown solutions, drive sustainable digital transformation, and inspire future tech leaders across the continent and beyond.

Past Speakers

  • Juliana Rotich – Co-founder of Ushahidi and an influential advocate for open-source technology and civic innovation in Africa.
  • Sam Altman – CEO of OpenAI, who shared visionary insights on AI and its potential to reshape the African digital economy.
  • John Kamara – Founder of AfyaRekod and AI Centre of Excellence Africa, championing healthtech innovation and data-driven solutions.
  • Rebecca Enonchong – Tech entrepreneur and founder of AppsTech, well-known for her work promoting digital inclusion and entrepreneurship in Africa..
